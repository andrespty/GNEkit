# Problem A4

A variant of Problem A3 where the diagonal terms of the $A^\nu$ matrices depend on the other players' variables, making the Jaco-Hessian neither constant nor positive (semi-)definite in general.

**Players:** $N = 3$ with $n_1 = 3$, $n_2 = 2$, $n_3 = 2$ variables respectively.

## Objective Functions

Same quadratic structure as Problem A3, but with $x$-dependent self-coupling matrices:

$$A^1 = \begin{pmatrix} 20 + (x^2_1)^2 & 5 & 3 \\ 5 & 5 + (x^2_2)^2 & -5 \\ 3 & -5 & 15 \end{pmatrix}, \quad A^2 = \begin{pmatrix} 11 + (x^3_1)^2 & -1 \\ -1 & 9 \end{pmatrix}, \quad A^3 = \begin{pmatrix} 48 & 39 \\ 39 & 53 + (x^1_1)^2 \end{pmatrix}$$

The $B^\nu$ and $b^\nu$ matrices are the same as in Problem A3.

## Constraints

Same structure as Problem A3, but with lower bounds set to $1$ (instead of $-10$) and upper bounds $10$.

## Known Solution

$$\bar{x} \approx (0.9998,\; 0.9999,\; 0.9999,\; 0.9999,\; 0.9998,\; 0.9999,\; 0.9999)^T$$

(All components close to 1.)

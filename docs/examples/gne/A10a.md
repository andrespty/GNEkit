# Problem A10a

A GNEP based on the **Arrow-Debreu competitive economy model** (Arrow and Debreu, 1954). This instance has $F=2$ firms, $C=5$ consumers, and $P=3$ goods ($N = F + C + 1 = 8$ players total).

## Players and Variables

- **$F = 2$ firm players**, each controlling $y^j \in \mathbb{R}^P$ (production quantities)
- **$C = 5$ consumer players**, each controlling $x^i \in \mathbb{R}^P$ (consumption quantities)
- **1 market player**, controlling the normalized price vector $p \in \mathbb{R}^P$

## Optimization Problems

**Firms** ($j = 1, \ldots, F$): maximize profit subject to a production capacity constraint:

$$\max_{y^j}\; p^T y^j \qquad \text{s.t.} \quad y^j \geq 0, \quad \sum_{k=1}^{P}(y^j_k)^2 \leq j \cdot 10$$

**Consumers** ($i = 1, \ldots, C$): maximize utility subject to a budget constraint:

$$\max_{x^i}\; u_i(x^i) \qquad \text{s.t.} \quad x^i \geq 0, \quad p^T x^i \leq p^T \xi^i$$

where $\xi^i \in \mathbb{R}^P_+$ is the initial endowment and the utility functions are quadratic:

$$u_i(x^i) = -\tfrac{1}{2}(x^i)^T Q^i x^i + (b^i)^T x^i$$

**Market player**: maximizes excess demand:

$$\max_{p}\; p^T\\!\left(\sum_{i=1}^C x^i - \sum_{j=1}^F y^j - \sum_{i=1}^C \xi^i\right) \qquad \text{s.t.} \quad p \geq 0, \quad \sum_{h=1}^{P} p_h = 1$$

For $i = 1, 2$:

$$Q^i = \begin{pmatrix} 6 & -2 & 5 \\ -2 & 6 & -7 \\ 5 & -7 & 20 \end{pmatrix}, \quad b^i = \begin{pmatrix} 30+i+F \\ 30+i+F \\ 30+i+F \end{pmatrix}, \quad \xi^i = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$$

For $i = 3, 4, 5$:

$$Q^i = \begin{pmatrix} 6 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 7 & -5 \\ 0 & -5 & 7 \end{pmatrix}, \quad b^i = \begin{pmatrix} 30+(i+F)\cdot 2 \\ 30+(i+F)\cdot 2 \\ 30+(i+F)\cdot 2 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \xi^i = \begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ 5 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$$

The algorithm is started from $y^j = 0$, $x^i = 0$, $p_k = 1/P$.
